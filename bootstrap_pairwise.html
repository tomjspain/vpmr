Recurrent event discrimination · Bootstrap vs Jackknife

Why bootstrap resampling introduces spurious txy pairs

Pairwise concordance statistics (Kendall's τ, Somers' D, C-statistic) are estimated over all pairs of individuals. Bootstrap resampling with replacement produces duplicate individuals — pairs involving copies of the same individual are forced into artificial ties on both observed and predicted counts.

Jackknife avoids this entirely. The grouped jackknife drops d individuals from the original sample in each replicate — no individual is ever duplicated, so the pairwise space contains only genuine pairs. This preserves the integrity of con, dis, t_x, t_y, and t_xy classifications.
